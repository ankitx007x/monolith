01. Classifications & Core Definitions

AUTOPSY (autos = self, opis = view): To see for oneself. Investigate dissection of a dead body[span_1](start_span)[span_1](end_span).

NEGATIVE AUTOPSY: When gross and microscopic examination, toxicological analysis, and laboratory investigations fail to reveal a cause of death (occurs in 2-5% of cases)[span_2](start_span)[span_2](end_span).

Medicolegal Indication Criteria

Autopsies are legally mandated under state law for sudden, suspicious, or unnatural scenarios[span_11](start_span)[span_11](end_span):

  • Accidents (Traffic, machinery, falls, animal attacks)[span_12](start_span)[span_12](end_span).
  • Assaults, sexual offences, criminal abortions[span_13](start_span)[span_13](end_span).
  • Burns, scalds, electrical injuries, poisoning, and substance intoxication[span_14](start_span)[span_14](end_span).
  • Comatose patients, cases brought in terminal states, or cases brought dead (Brought Dead / BID)[span_15](start_span)[span_15](end_span).
  • Suicide, homicide, infanticide, or sudden unexpected deaths[span_16](start_span)[span_16](end_span).
  • Deaths during or under anesthesia, drug mishaps, or frankly criminal deaths[span_17](start_span)[span_17](end_span).

02. Regulatory Rules for Autopsies in Nepal

  • Labeling: Bodies must be labeled immediately upon arrival at the mortuary facility[span_18](start_span)[span_18](end_span).
  • Jurisdiction: Conducted strictly under an official written order from the police or Chief District Officer (CDO in Nepal)[span_19](start_span)[span_19](end_span).
  • Location & Practitioner: Performed only in authorized government hospitals by a registered medical practitioner trained in forensic medicine. Private room exams are strictly forbidden[span_20](start_span)[span_20](end_span).
  • Daylight Rule: Examination must be done in natural daylight whenever possible[span_21](start_span)[span_21](end_span). If received at night, perform a preliminary exam (note external state, temperature, rigor, lividity) and defer internal dissection until morning[span_22](start_span)[span_22](end_span).
  • Document Integrity: No text erasures or alterations are allowed on the protocol. Errors must be initialed and cleanly struck through[span_23](start_span)[span_23](end_span). Both positive and negative findings must be logged[span_24](start_span)[span_24](end_span).
  • Report Policy: The finalized report is a confidential legal document submitted directly to investigating officers; it is never handed over to the private party or relatives[span_25](start_span)[span_25](end_span).

03. Surgical Skin Incisions

I-Shaped Incision

The swiftest and most preferred type of incision[span_26](start_span)[span_26](end_span). Extends from the chin straight down the midline to just above the umbilicus, deviates laterally around the left or right side of the umbilicus, and extends down to the symphysis pubis[span_27](start_span)[span_27](end_span).

Viva Rationale: The umbilicus is strictly bypassed because its dense fibrous tissue is exceptionally difficult to cleanly penetrate with standard surgical needles during post-exam reconstruction[span_28](start_span)[span_28](end_span).

Y-Shaped Incision

Begins close to both acromion processes, sweeps down symmetrically beneath the breasts to the xiphoid process, and continues down the midline to the symphysis pubis[span_29](start_span)[span_29](end_span). Chosen to preserve cosmetic appearance of the anterior chest wall for viewing or specific religious customs (e.g., coffin funerals)[span_30](start_span)[span_30](end_span).

Modified Y-Shaped Incision

Begins at the suprasternal notch, runs down to the pubis, with superior extensions starting from the supraclavicular notches, passing over the center of the clavicles, and upward over the neck behind the ears[span_31](start_span)[span_31](end_span).

Mandatory for Hanging & Strangulation Cases: Allows the neck vessels to drain empty first, creating a completely dry, bloodless field during deep neck structure dissection[span_32](start_span)[span_32](end_span). This prevents blood artifacts from masking crucial airway or ligature patterns[span_33](start_span)[span_33](end_span).

04. Internal Dissection Protocols

Cranial Cavity (Skull & Brain)

  1. Positioning: Place a wooden block beneath the shoulders to extend the neck, securing the head inside a semicircular groove rest[span_34](start_span)[span_34](end_span).
  2. Scalp Incision: Run a coronal intermastoidal incision starting behind one mastoid process, continuing over the vertex to the opposite ear[span_35](start_span)[span_35](end_span). Reflect the scalp flap forward to the supraciliary ridges and backward past the occipital protuberance[span_36](start_span)[span_36](end_span).
  3. Vault Removal: Cut the skull cap with a bone saw along a slightly V-shaped track so that it cleanly locks back into position during body reconstruction[span_37](start_span)[span_37](end_span).
  4. Dura and Brain Extraction: Examine the intact dura for extra/subdural clots[span_38](start_span)[span_38](end_span). Open the superior longitudinal sinus to rule out antemortem thrombi[span_39](start_span)[span_39](end_span). Cut optic nerves, reflect tentorium cerebelli, sever the spinal cord as low as possible through the foramen magnum, and carefully support and extract the brain[span_40](start_span)[span_40](end_span).
  5. Brain Sectioning: Slice the brain into two symmetric halves through the corpus callosum and brainstem[span_41](start_span)[span_41](end_span). Cut serial transverse sections 1 cm apart from the frontal lobe back to the occiput to screen deep structures[span_42](start_span)[span_42](end_span).

Visceral Removal Techniques

  • Virchow’s Technique: Organs are removed one by one (Cranial → Thoracic → Cervical → Abdominal). Anatomico-pathological structural relationships are lost[span_43](start_span)[span_43](end_span).
  • Rokitansky’s Technique: In-situ dissection followed by en-block organ removal. Preferred for highly infectious exposures (e.g., HIV, Hepatitis B) and is the standard method for infants[span_44](start_span)[span_44](end_span).
  • Letulle’s Technique: Cervical, thoracic, abdominal, and pelvic visceral systems are removed entirely en masse, keeping all anatomical attachments fully intact as a single block[span_45](start_span)[span_45](end_span).
  • Ghon’s Technique: Organs are extracted as localized, specialized organ blocks (e.g., Cervico-thoraco-abdominal block vs. separate neural block)[span_46](start_span)[span_46](end_span).

Cardiovascular System Protocol

Dissection requires precise assessment of three primary components: coronary patency, chamber parameters, and myocardial structural integrity[span_47](start_span)[span_47](end_span).

  1. Perform serial cross-sections 2–3 mm apart along the entire course of the major coronary vessels using a scalpel to track patency or thrombi[span_48](start_span)[span_48](end_span).
  2. Open heart chambers using the inflow-outflow method with an enterotome[span_49](start_span)[span_49](end_span).
  3. Cut the right atrium between the vena cavae, inspect the appendage for thrombi, pass through the tricuspid valve into the right ventricle, and extend out parallel to the interventricular septum into the pulmonary trunk[span_50](start_span)[span_50](end_span).
  4. Open the left atrium along its lateral wall, extend through the mitral valve to the left ventricular apex, and cut upwards along the septum into the aortic valve[span_51](start_span)[span_51](end_span). Measure valve circumferences using graduated cones before making incisions[span_52](start_span)[span_52](end_span).
  5. Log wall thickness profiles, remove all clots, weigh the heart, and slice the myocardium parallel to the epicardial surface to expose hidden infarcts or deep fibrosis[span_53](start_span)[span_53](end_span).
Viva Distinction: Thrombi vs. Clots
• Antemortem Thrombus: Dark red, firm, friable, and strictly attached to the vessel endothelial wall. Characterized by alternate microscopic fibrin/platelet bands (Lines of Zahn)[span_54](start_span)[span_54](end_span).
• Postmortem Clot: Moist, smooth, shiny, rubbery, and completely unattached[span_55](start_span)[span_55](end_span). May show uniform "Red Currant Jelly" configurations or sedimented "Chicken Fat" layers (bright yellow plasma/serum layered cleanly over settled red cell bases)[span_56](start_span)[span_56](end_span).

05. Applied Forensic Scenario (High-Yield Case)

Case: Full-Term Obstructed Labor (Maternal & Fetal Demise)

MATERNAL POST-MORTEM PROFILE (Mother)[span_57](start_span)[span_57](end_span)
• External Findings: Marked signs of profound exhaustion and systemic dehydration; dry mucous membranes; significant vulval and vaginal edema with focal birth canal lacerations or bruising. A distinct Bandl’s ring (pathological uterine retraction ring) may be visibly indented across the lower abdominal wall.
• Internal Findings: Complete uterine rupture (most commonly localized to the thinner lower uterine segment) accompanied by massive intraperitoneal hemorrhage. Alternatively, extensive ischemic necrosis of the dense uterine muscle wall is noted. The fetal presenting part remains tightly wedged into the bony pelvic brim.
• Cause of Death: Acute Hemorrhagic Shock (secondary to catastrophic uterine rupture) OR Septic Shock (secondary to prolonged chorioamnionitis).
• Manner of Death: Natural.
• Mechanism of Death: Acute cardiovascular collapse or rapid Multiorgan Dysfunction Syndrome (MODS).
FETAL POST-MORTEM PROFILE (Baby)[span_58](start_span)[span_58](end_span)
• External Findings: Severe distortion, overlapping molding of the skull bones, and prominent caput succedaneum or cephalhematoma. Focal skin peeling or maceration artifacts may present depending on the exact intrauterine post-mortem interval.
• Internal Findings: Extensive subdural or subarachnoid intracranial hemorrhages resulting from extreme mechanical cranial bone overriding. Classic widespread systemic asphyxial markings are present: pronounced visceral petechiae (Tardieu spots over visceral pleura and pericardial membranes) with thick, tenacious meconium aspiration obstructing the trachea and primary bronchi.

06. Viscera Collection & Toxicology Preservatives

Viscera must be preserved if poisoning or intoxication is suspected, if the cause of death is completely obscure after basic autopsy, or in anaphylactic, burn, or decomposition cases[span_59](start_span)[span_59](end_span). Fill wide-mouthed glass jars only to two-thirds capacity to prevent burst hazards from decomposition gases[span_60](start_span)[span_60](end_span).

07. Forensic Artifacts (High-Yield Viva)

An artifact is any post-mortem modification or finding introduced into the body that risks being misinterpreted as a significant antemortem pathological lesion[span_75](start_span)[span_75](end_span).

I. Pre-Autopsy Phase Artifacts

  • Agonal Artifacts: Asphyxia can cause terminal vomiting, filling the airways with gastric contents; this can be mistaken as a primary cause of accidental choking/asphyxiation[span_76](start_span)[span_76](end_span). Oesophagogastromalacia (autodigestion via leaked gastric enzymes) can mimic corrosive ingestion[span_77](start_span)[span_77](end_span).
  • Resuscitation Defects: Intramuscular injection tracks, linear rib fractures, or precordial contusions from cardiac massage can mimic antemortem violence; defibrillator plates leave ring-like skin contusions[span_78](start_span)[span_78](end_span).
  • Rigor Mortis Artifacts: Intense rigor affecting the myocardium can simulate concentric left ventricular hypertrophy[span_79](start_span)[span_79](end_span). Rough body transport handling can prematurely fracture rigor bonds[span_80](start_span)[span_80](end_span).
  • Thermal Artifacts: Intense heat ruptures on skin can closely resemble incised or lacerated wounds[span_81](start_span)[span_81](end_span). Heat hematomas inside the cranium can easily simulate classic traumatic extradural hemorrhages[span_82](start_span)[span_82](end_span).
  • Anthropophagy (Animal/Insect Bites): Post-mortem domestic dog bites can mimic sharp stab wounds[span_83](start_span)[span_83](end_span). Dense linear ant pathways tracking across the neck skin can mimic mechanical ligature abrasions[span_84](start_span)[span_84](end_span).

II. Intra-Autopsy Phase Artifacts

  • Air Embolism Simulation: Forcefully pulling the dura mater along the sagittal line can artificially draw environmental air directly into the superior longitudinal sinus, creating a false air embolism presentation[span_85](start_span)[span_85](end_span).
  • Iatrogenic Bone Fractures: Aggressive, forceful leverage when removing the sawed skull cap can cause artificial linear skull fractures[span_86](start_span)[span_86](end_span). Hard, improper manipulation of the larynx can fracture the delicate hyoid bone[span_87](start_span)[span_87](end_span).
